Lyu H et al. demonstrated that Alphafold 3 can be used to predict the binding motif of the Stimulator of interferon genes (STING) with the SEC24 homolog C (SEC24C). Based on the structural data, they found the STING protein has an ER-exit signal peptide "EEΦxΦ", which binds to the IxM-binding site of the SEC24C protein. The SEC24C IxM motif also binds to various cargo proteins, like the non-LEE-encoded effector A (NleA), Sytaxin-5 (STX5), Membrin (GOSR2), and Reticulon-3 (RTN3), which forms a longer betasheet than that of the STING protein. As the COP-II mediated trafficking from ER to Golgi is essential for the activation of STING, they concluded that the STING protein adopts a sub-optimal ER-exit signal peptide to avoid overactivation. Such findings inspire them to engineer the NleA ER-exit signal to replace of the original STING peptide to enhance the antitumor activity.
