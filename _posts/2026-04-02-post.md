Rodriguez GE and Zhao Y et al. developed a synthetic three-chain cytokine complex, in which a base cytokine is linked to a non-signalling binder (e.g. a VHH) that recruit additional receptor to form ternary cytokine receptor complexes that compel the simultaneous activation of specific STAT signalling pathways. They called such therapeutics "trikines", which are not dual agonists of the upstream STAT receptors, but rather an AND-gate agonist. 

<img src="/assets/images/trikine.jpg"> 

IL-2-mediated pSTAT5 signalling facilitates cell proliferation and survival, while persistent pSTAT5 signalling can drive terminal differentiation and exhaustion. Alternatively, IL-21 and IL-10-mediated pSTAT3 signalling promote stem-like, less exhausted T cells with minimal proliferation. Therefore, it would be beneficial to activate both pathways simultaneously. Instead of fusing two cytokines directly, which could activate cells that express only one of the receptors, the Trikines actions are restricted to cells co-expressing three receptors to simultaneously deliver both pSTAT5 and pSTAT3 from one ternary signalling complex, thus limiting its toxicity caused by the NK cell dominantly.


<h3>Questions to be addressed</h3>
1. What are the long-term safety and immunogenicity profiles of Trikines in vivo?
2. How might variability in cytokine receptor expression among patients or tumor types affect Trikine efficacy?
3. What are the effects of Trikines in the context of complex human immune systems or in the presence of comorbidities?
