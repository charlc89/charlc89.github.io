Liu S, Liu MR, Wang ZZ et al. developed an inhalable exosome, termed bispecific exosome activator of T cells (BEAT), which displays two inhibitory checkpoint ligands to treat immune checkpoint inhibitor-resistant melanoma. The two inhibitory checkpoint ligands include the PD-1 and FZD8 conjugated with Alix1 sorting domain, that blocks the PD-L1 and Wnt7b signalling. Such an exosome system reprogrammes the tumour microenvironment via recruiting and activating CD8+ T cells in the tumour. 


<h3>Questions to be addressed</h3>
1. How can the BEAT platform be adapted or combined with other therapies to address tumours driven by resistance pathways other than Wnt/β-catenin signalling?
2. What other delivery methods could be developed to target tumours in organs other than the lungs, and how might these methods affect the efficacy and safety of BEAT?
3. How might BEAT be combined with other immunotherapies or targeted therapies to enhance its efficacy and overcome resistance in a broader range of cancers?
