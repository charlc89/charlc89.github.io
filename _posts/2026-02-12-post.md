  ## Engineering bispecific exosome activators of T cells to target immune checkpoint inhibitor-resistant metastatic melanoma
feature_image: "https://picsum.photos/2560/600?image=733"
image: "https://picsum.photos/2560/600?image=733"

Liu S, Liu MR, Wang ZZ et al. developed an inhalable exosome, termed bispecific exosome activator of T cells (BEAT), which displays two inhibitory checkpoint ligands to treat immune checkpoint inhibitor-resistant melanoma. The two inhibitory checkpoint ligands include the PD-1 and FZD8 conjugated with Alix1 sorting domain, that blocks the PD-L1 and Wnt7b signalling. Such an exosome system reprogrammes the tumour microenvironment via recruiting and activating CD8+ T cells in the tumour.
